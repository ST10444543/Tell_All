About Us

Transforming Lives with Education

"Tell All" is a non-profit organization dedicated to addressing the profound gender inequalities and lack of access to education that affect millions of girls and young women in sub-Saharan Africa. The region has an average literacy rate of approximately 65%, but these numbers are even lower for girls in many countries. The disparity in access to education between boys and girls is alarming, and "Tell All" aims to change this reality.

Our commitment is founded on the belief that education is a fundamental human right and a powerful tool for breaking the cycle of poverty and inequality that affects many communities in sub-Saharan Africa. By investing in girls' education, we transform lives and improve the well-being of families and communities.

Justification

In a world where gender inequality continues to hinder social and economic progress, "Tell All" stands as a powerful advocate for change. Educational inequality between girls and boys is a persistent issue, and the UN warns that it will take 140 years for women to achieve equal representation in all areas of life. This underscores the impact of deep-rooted cultural norms, underinvestment in girls' education, and gender-based violence.

Creating a "Tell All" website is not just an outreach tool; it is essential for amplifying our message, increasing global visibility, and mobilizing resources. A well-designed website will help us reach a wider audience, including potential donors, partners, and volunteers, while providing an accessible platform for girls and families in need of our services.

Our Commitment

Mission, Vision, and Purpose

At "Tell All," we believe that every girl deserves the chance to reach her full potential. Our mission is to promote education and empowerment, ensuring that all girls have access to life-changing opportunities.

Mission:

To promote education and empower girls by creating opportunities for them to reach their full potential in vulnerable communities.

Vision:

A world where all girls have access to quality education and are free to build the future they want.

Objective:

To ensure that every girl in our communities has access to the education, psychological support, and tools needed to overcome gender barriers and achieve her dreams.
